What enables GPT to work?

  • GPT-4 builds on the success of scale.
  • Scale. Data and model size.
  • But scale has a ceiling.
  • Scaling works because we already have so much data it can go through. And it’s all been generated by humans with the intention of conveying information to human readers.

What types of training does GPT go through?

  • It goes through two types of training.
    • One, foundational, for knowledge storage and the potential for reasoning abilities.
    • And another, added later, for enabling the query-response mechanism and applying high-level alignment restrictions.
  • LM training which enables the knowledge base and reasoning abilities.
    • Needs very big data.
  • Instruct training which develops/enables/surfaces the Query-Response mechanism.
  • RLF training which enables high-level style/form/moral/??? alignment.
    • Like politeness or safety or legal awareness.
    • And then a company like Anthropic comes and says. Okay, maybe we’ll use these already good LMs so help us generate even more data for the alignment step, like for RLHF, and we end up with RLAIF which sounds great but… It’s still dependent on scaling. And it still depends on the implicit promise that the generalization seen from these deep, correlational, semi-supervised models is the kind of generalization that applies logically to all relevant interpretations of the symbols we’re training it on. But what actually happens is that we need RLAIF BECAUSE we can’t that guarantee that from only a few human examples, but can still measure it statistically.
      • Applying the alignment as input to the model session (which, in ChatGPT, would be in its memory module) highlights another fundamental shortcoming of this whole design: It can’t learn incrementally as part of the interaction, only by explicit tuning steps. Yes? No? No news from OpenAI yet.
    • And more data plays into that strength of scaling by optimizing the implicit function to a point of more closely approximating the intended symbolic alignment tat the human aligners wanted to express through RLHF examples and later through RLAIF constitutional directives.
    • And then a company like Anthropic comes up with a way to automate this alignment effort (RLHF -> RLAIF), again approaching “scale” for the solution.

What enables GPT training in text mode?

  • Very big data, already largely available. Like 10^9+ data size.
  • If you reduced the amount of training data in the LM type training to a size that would be manageable for a human to go through within, say, 20 years;
    • It would be very unlikely to arrive at a model with the same reasoning abilities as GPT.
    • And it would be, understandably, impossible to arrive at a model with the same knowledge repository as GPT.
  • The intent guiding the creation of this human text data is communication of meaning to other humans. The data is meant to be understandable and convey meaning efficiently.
    • Unlike videos, which most of the time are meant to do that implicitly.

Text versus visual modalities

  • Even in visual data, there is an implicit language
    • But it seems to be much more complex than text
  • Take the example of illustrations
    • The poses, composition of colors, layout, object placement and scene composition, as well as many subtle cues inherent in the cultural context of the generation all together enable the conveyance of meaning to the viewer.
  • A text description of a table may be able to convey its appearance efficiently. Or possibly its manufacture. But different layers of such meaning need different level of detail to be conveyed.
    • Conveying such detail is a trade-off between compression efficiency and clarity of the tokens/atoms used in the description.
      • Given a shared cultural or instance context between 2 people, more compressed descriptions may be able to convey the same meaning that would need many more tokens in a communication between 2 people with no shared context.
      • That’s why the table itself may be able to contain the details of its making and history very efficiently by being there, but interpreting those details requires someone who already possesses significant context about the table.
      • In contrast, a text description using atoms/tokens with more standardized meaning may be able to convey the details of its manufacturing or history more precisely to more varied listeners on average.
      • But it would be much more verbose the deeper we go into requirements of clarifying certain intentions in the text that do not come, implicitly, from an external shared context. Thus a description using a living human language may be 200 words long when written for a trained carpenter, but 2000 words long when written to a generally educated layman.
    • Thus the purpose of text is to convey specific meaning given specific grounding context, which may come from other text or from different modalities.
      • In contrast, a video or a painting is, generally, not meant to stand alone as a clear message of meaning using a standardized language. Without the grounding context, which can many layers deep, only the most common layers or the most superficial can be expectedly learned by a semi-supervised method.

Outline

  • GPT-4 rumors
  • Multi-modality may not work as expected
  • GPT-like models are enabled by scale, at both the model and data/training levels
  • The data that enables both knowledge abilities and reasoning in GPT-like models is text data that was written intentionally to convey information efficiently and relatively precisely to a human reader. There lies a possibly infinite set of sub-languages in such data, each encoding the knowledge and algorithms of its respective domain in such a way that enables LM-style training to actually learn those domain-specific skills and knowledge. But it does so only statistically.
  • Then consider what it means for a model like GPT to be “usable”.
  • There are two types of such training. One is unsupervised for the foundation of knowledge and reasoning, and one is supervised (even if partially based on RL) for the interactivity and alignment.
    1. There’s the underlying unsupervised LM training which gives it the knowledge and the bedrock for its reasoning abilities.
    2. Then there’s the Instruct training that helps develop the query-to-response mechanism.
    3. Then a feedback alignment stage, for example with RL, is used to align the form and intent of its responses at a high level according to an interactivity design put forward by its alignment team.
  • So these models see something to the tune of 10^9 tokens per LM training. But far less for Instruct/RLHF training. So scale is definitely a big factor. So a company like Anthropic comes along and they figure out how to use LMs where they shine, to generate examples that they can use for the alignment step, based on some very high-level statements comprising their “constitution” in Constitutional AI.
  • With that in mind and back to the first point, let’s compare to a human. A GPT-like model possesses knowledge far beyond what any single human could ever hope to ingest. If we look from the opposite side of the comparison, what would a model do in a human’s shoe? What would be a model trained with at most as much text data as a human can ingest in a 20 year period?
    • It can be seen that such a model could not possibly access as many distinct pieces of knowledge as its unshackled sibling. Therefore its pretrained knowledge repository is understandably much, much smaller.
    • Then, we claim, its reasoning abilities will nearly disappear in light of the reduced overall data size.
      • Unless another training method or change can enable it to do that in spite of the reduced data, and to apply it to further learning, then effectively…
      • … this has robbed it of its second pillar–reasoning. And so considering it in the same light as a fully trained LLM is unwise.
  • Everything, in some sense, can be reduced to a language. Even visually. Illustrations can be…
    • Scene composition and cultural context.
    • The meaning conveyed relies on a shared definition of the atoms, or tokens, used in the description.
    • In illustrations, these descriptions can be very complex and in multiple layers, with most implicit and interpretable only through that shared context that the viewer shares with the painter.
    • This context is part of being a human member of society. As time goes and people live, this context changes and the meaning and interpretation change with it (although a lot of it is preserved still in a sort of psychological archive, and can be inferred slightly from other media and data on the period or location pertaining to the piece in question).
    • Compare to the understanding of a model. The meaning, and the structure of its depiction, could be inferred statistically from observing many illustrations within similar cultural tag groups (like fan-made, or revolutionary, or pertaining to a particular time period, or group of people, or a job, or a feeling, or a place, and so on). But that inference comes from a statistical process that, we claim, would fail to observe past a very high, or superficial level of meaning. And would do so only based on recorded human descriptions of similar works. In essence, the kind of information that humans would record about a media may or may not convey information about said media deeper than a certain level of nuance. If they convey only higher-level meaning, then they do so because the rest is left to the viewer to interpret on their own. If they go into lower-level meaning, then they do so from a very particular position as a specific individual or ego making that description. Learning from it as a way to learn to interpret such pieces in the absence of many of the mechanisms that allow a human individual to make their own corresponding interpretation of the same piece means that any output of such a model, in true LM fashion, is meaningless parroting. And it is so precisely because the meaning here is not merely the organization of a set of atoms or tokens in a correct way to convey composable blocks of standardized meaning, but it also depends intentionally on the viewers own, internally consistent model which supplies the context in which the piece is meant to be interpreted, as a carrier of partial signals of meaning lower in their specificity than the more rigid and atomic structure of texts.
