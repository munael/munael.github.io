There Are No Fakes: AI-Generated Content Is Undetectable

Muhammad ElNokrashy January 2023
What makes data "real"? AI-generated content may not be discernable a priori, but only within a syntactic and epistemologic context.

The Brief

  • The overwhelming majority of modern mainstream machine learning models are almost fully statistical {citation needed}.
  • Early ML generation detectors focus on form (syntax, structure, visual quality, ...).
  • As generators improve, observers begin to note plausibility, within a "domain of expectations".
  • Detection by implausibility falls into two traps:
    1. The distribution of plausible data is often unknown.
      • The context to clarify it is often implicit or inaccessible.
    2. Arbitrary data may be coherent in different contexts.
      • Noticeably incoherent data is already caught by the form detectors.
      • Without strict structure and clear contexts, the detector becomes arbitrary.

The Setup

  • Statistical self-supervised machine learning models are made to learn to interpolate.
  • Given an appropriate objective design, the model can be tricked at runtime to attempt to extrapolate. With impressive results.
  • The model may learn a realistic interpolation space that approximates the true, probably hidden distribution of a communication medium as used by a culture.
  • Data generated by such models can pass as authentic to an examiner according to different criteria, such as:
    1. Validity of form. Syntax, Structure, etc.
    2. Plausibility of content. May include judgement on truth-values, perceived arbitrariness, and so on.
    3. Verifiable authorship. Through cryptographic signatures, trust networks, auditing of work/toil, process transparency...
      • For example when judging the authority of a video released by an account marked as "official" by the hosting platform.
